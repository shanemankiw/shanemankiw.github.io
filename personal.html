Research

My current research interests lie in the intersection of 3D vision and Graphics, specifically in neural rendering, digital human, animation, etc.

Efficient Human Rendering with Geometric and Semantic Priors
Jionghao Wang, Shuai Guo, Rong Xie, Li Song
To Appear in IEEE BMSB 2023

We propose an effective human rendering pipeline, generating geometric and semantic guidances as priors to further improve the pipeline's effectiveness and quality. In particular, a semantic human part parsing guides pixel sampling in 2D space, while a mesh prior guides an occupancy field for efficient ray sampling in 3D space.

RGBD-based Real-time Volumetric Reconstruction System: Architecture Design and Implementation
Kai Zhou, Shuai Guo, Jingchuan Hu, Jionghao Wang, Qiuwen Wang, Li Song
VCIP '22

In this paper, a low-cost, real-time system: LiveRecon3D, is presented, with multiple RGB-D cameras connected to one single computer. The goal of the system is to provide an interactive frame rate for 3D content capture and rendering at a reduced cost.

A New Free Viewpoint Video Dataset and DIBR Benchmark
Shuai Guo, Kai Zhou, Jingchuan Hu, Jionghao Wang, Jun Xu, Li Song
MMSys '22

In this paper, we present a new dynamic RGB-D video dataset with up to 12 views. Our dataset consists of 13 groups of dynamic video sequences, taken in the same scene.

Projects

Below are some of my recent projects. Some older projects of mine could be found on my Github.

Depth Image Based Rendering

We used a rather traditional Depth-Image Based Rendering algorithm, which performs interpolation by warping neighboring view RGB values to novel view based on depth values, and then blend them together. The system is optimized and can run in real-time.

Learning-based Multiview Stereo

We mimicked the traditional Patchmatch-based algorithm for dense multi-view stereo with a designed deep neural network. Given neighboring view images, we estimate the dense depth value of a given view by running patchmatch modules and depth refinement modules iteratively.

Kinect Fusion Pipeline

We fuse multiple Kinect sensors RGB-D inputs, generating a world-space point cloud, and refine the pipeline using masks and depth refinement. Specifically for depth refinement, we proposed to add another constraint to the classic joint bilateral kernel, and achieves better results on Kinect data.
